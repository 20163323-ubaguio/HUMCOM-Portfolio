ABOUT ME

I am a creative professional and IT student at the University of Baguio, where I am bridging the gap between physical design and digital innovation. My journey began in architecture, which instilled in me a deep appreciation for structure, drafting, and aesthetic balance. Today, I apply those same principles to Information Technology—focusing on web development, programming, and hardware troubleshooting. For me, building a website is much like designing a building: it requires a solid foundation, intuitive flow, and a human-centered touch.

Why Information Technology?

Information Technology wasn’t my first choice, but it has become my greatest pursuit. My journey into this field was born out of a simple, persistent curiosity: How does it work, and how can I fix it? That curiosity evolved into a passion for troubleshooting hardware, the logic of programming, and the creative challenge of building websites. For me, choosing IT was a decision of passion over practicality. I’m not just learning to code; I’m learning to build, solve, and innovate in a digital landscape that is constantly evolving.

Why the University of Baguio?

Coming back to the University of Baguio as a returning student was a decision made from the heart. While it wasn't my first choice initially, my first year here completely changed my perspective. I found a vibrant, fun environment and met incredible people who shaped my journey. Returning to this campus has given me the unique opportunity to reconnect with friends—seeing them reach their graduation milestone while I embark on my new path in IT. For me, UB isn't just a school; it's the community that inspired me to come back and finish strong.

Achievement

Through my studies in Human-Computer Interaction, I mastered the art of balancing user-centered design with digital accessibility, learning to transform complex technical requirements into intuitive, meaningful experiences that bridge the gap between human emotion and machine precision.

Goals

My future goal in Human-Computer Interaction is to design inclusive, human-centric interfaces that harmonize aesthetic elegance with accessibility, ensuring technology feels natural and empowering for users of all backgrounds and abilities.

Reflection

Studying Human-Computer Interaction has fundamentally shifted my perspective, teaching me that the most successful technology is not the most complex, but the one that most deeply understands and respects the diverse needs, emotions, and limitations of the human beings using it.
